1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how ChronicOS (“the App”) collects, uses, discloses, retains, and protects information when you download, access, or use the App. ChronicOS is a personal health organization tool for adults managing multiple chronic conditions. It is not a medical device, healthcare provider, or emergency service.

By using the App, you agree to this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, do not use the App. Where required by law, we will request your consent before certain processing (for example, optional analytics or personalization).

2. Who We Are

ChronicOS is published and operated by an individual software developer (not a hospital, insurer, or clinical practice). We are the data controller for information processed through the App, except where a third party acts as an independent controller (for example, Apple for App Store payments, or your chosen health system for portal data you authorize).

Privacy contact: hello@familymedvault.com

3. Summary (Plain Language)

  • Health data is stored primarily on your device. You control export and deletion from Settings.
  • We do not sell your personal information or protected health information (PHI).
  • We do not use your data for third-party advertising or cross-app tracking for ads.
  • Many features are free (symptom and medication tracking, timelines, rule-based alerts, medication interaction scanning, doctor visit records, between-visit coaching).
  • Optional paid features use auto-renewable subscriptions and consumable “Health AI Points” processed by Apple (and Google Play on Android).
  • AI outputs are informational only and are not medical advice.
  • Cloud sync, FHIR portal import, and caregiver sharing are optional and only occur when you enable them.

4. Information We Collect

4.1 Information you provide

  • Profile information (such as display name) and active chronic condition selections
  • Health and wellness data you enter or import, including symptoms, pain/fatigue levels, medications (prescription, OTC, supplements), side-effect notes, lab results, vital signs, doctor and visit records, visit-preparation notes, and timeline entries
  • Caregiver collaboration data if you invite a caregiver or accept an invite (permissions you set, observations logged on your behalf)
  • Account credentials if you sign in for cloud backup or sync (e.g., email; authentication tokens stored securely on device)
  • Consent choices at first launch: optional anonymous analytics, optional personalization of coaching tips, and region selection (United States or EEA/GDPR-oriented)
  • Communications you send to us (support email)

4.2 Information collected automatically

  • App preferences (language, theme, notification settings)
  • Device-related technical data necessary to operate the App (operating system version, app version, general device type)
  • Anonymous usage analytics only if you opt in on the consent screen (you may leave this off)
  • Crash or diagnostic logs only as permitted by your device settings and applicable platform policies

4.3 Information from third parties (only if you connect them)

  • Apple App Store / Google Play: subscription and in-app purchase status, transaction identifiers (we do not receive your full payment card number)
  • Patient portal / FHIR: health records you authorize from your health system
  • Sign-in providers (if enabled): basic account identifiers per their policies
  • AI service providers (if you use paid AI features): portions of data you submit for processing to generate summaries, explanations, or structured entries—only for that request, subject to our configurations and applicable law

4.4 What we do not collect by design

  • We do not require Social Security numbers, insurance member IDs, or government IDs to use core features
  • We do not collect data for behavioral advertising networks
  • We do not access Apple HealthKit or Google Health Connect unless a future App version discloses that separately and requests your permission

6. How We Use Information

We use information only for legitimate purposes related to the App:

  • Provide core features: multi-condition dashboard, symptom diary, medication list and safety scanning, lab trends, visit preparation, between-visit coaching, alerts, PDF export, and doctor records
  • Operate optional AI-assisted features that consume Health AI Points (e.g., appointment prep, provider summaries, lab interpretation, voice structuring, longitudinal analysis)
  • Process subscriptions and consumable point packs through platform billing
  • Enable caregiver access according to permissions you configure
  • Sync or back up data to our servers only when you sign in and enable cloud features
  • Send local notifications (e.g., medication reminders, visit prep) if you allow notifications
  • Personalize coaching tips only if you opt in to personalization
  • Improve reliability and security; analyze opt-in anonymous usage trends
  • Respond to support requests and legal obligations

We do not sell or share your information for third-party advertising.

7. Legal Bases (EEA/UK Users)

If you select an EEA/GDPR-oriented region on the consent screen, we rely on:

  • Consent for optional analytics and personalization
  • Contract to provide features you request (including paid features you purchase)
  • Legitimate interests to secure the App, prevent abuse, and improve stability (balanced against your rights)
  • Legal obligation where applicable

You may withdraw consent for optional processing in Settings or by contacting us. Withdrawal does not affect lawfulness of prior processing.

8. When We Disclose Information

We may disclose information only in these situations:

  • Service providers that host APIs, databases, or AI inference under written confidentiality and security obligations (no sale of data)
  • Caregivers you invite, limited to the permission level you assign
  • Apple and Google for payment processing and subscription management
  • Health systems you authorize when you connect FHIR or patient-portal import
  • When you export or share PDFs or files from the App
  • Legal requirements (valid subpoena, court order, or to protect safety and rights)
  • Business transfer if the App or assets are acquired, subject to notice where required by law (Operator remains an individual developer unless otherwise stated)

We do not disclose PHI to advertisers or data brokers.

9. Storage, Location, and Retention

9.1 On-device storage

Most health records are stored locally on your phone or tablet using the App’s on-device database. You may export all local data as JSON or delete all data from Settings. Deletion on the device is permanent for that device’s local copy.

9.2 Cloud storage

If you enable sign-in and cloud sync/backup, copies may be stored on servers in the United States or other regions where our providers operate. We use HTTPS in transit and industry-standard safeguards at rest where supported by our infrastructure.

9.3 Retention

  • Local data: until you delete it or uninstall the App
  • Cloud data: until you delete your account/data or we no longer need it to provide the service
  • Support emails: as long as needed to resolve your request and comply with law
  • Purchase records: as required by tax and App Store rules

10. Security

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards appropriate to the nature of the data. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure. You are responsible for protecting your device (passcode, biometrics) and account credentials.

Production note: Current MVP builds may use local storage; production releases should use encrypted storage (e.g., iOS Keychain / encrypted database). This Policy describes our practices as implemented and planned for production.

11. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your location, you may have rights to:

  • Access and receive a copy of your data (in-app export)
  • Delete local data (in-app delete all data)
  • Correct information by editing it in the App
  • Opt out of optional analytics and personalization (consent screen or Settings)
  • Object or restrict certain processing (EEA/UK)
  • Portability (GDPR JSON export)
  • Complain to a supervisory authority (EEA/UK)

California residents may have rights under the CPRA (know, delete, correct, opt out of sale/share—we do not sell or share for cross-context behavioral advertising). Submit requests to hello@familymedvault.com. We will verify your request as required by law.

Apple users: You may also use App Store subscription management to cancel auto-renewal. Deleting the App does not automatically cancel subscriptions.

12. Children
